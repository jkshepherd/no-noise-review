Edition 01 — Carabao Cup — Etihad Stadium

Man City 3–1 Newcastle United

February 4, 2026

Newcastle arrived in Manchester with the arithmetic already against them, two goals adrift from the first leg and little margin for hesitation. Any early belief was curtailed inside six minutes when Omar Marmoush slipped between defenders and saw a deflected effort loop beyond Aaron Ramsdale. The goalkeeper recovered swiftly to deny Tijjani Reijnders with a sharp save, but the tone of the evening had been set — City requiring few chances to extend their advantage.

There were openings for Newcastle to redraw the line. Joe Willock and Anthony Gordon both found themselves smothered at close range by James Trafford, moments that carried the weight of opportunity rather than spectacle. City answered with efficiency. Marmoush rose unmarked near the half-hour to head in a second, and before the interval Reijnders collected a loose ball and finished cleanly to place the tie beyond reasonable reach.

The second half unfolded with less urgency than confirmation. Anthony Elanga’s curling strike on sixty-two minutes offered Newcastle a measure of pride, while Harvey Barnes later saw an effort ruled out, but the contest itself had already settled into procession. City advanced with composure, the home support turning its attention toward Wembley long before the final whistle.

Final Whistle: An early deflection, two first-half finishes, and a deficit too steep to reclaim — Newcastle’s response carried spirit, but City’s precision had long since written the outcome.

Goalscorers

Man City: Marmoush (6’, 29’), Reijnders (43’)

Newcastle United: Elanga (62’)

Player of the Match

Omar Marmoush
